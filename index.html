Stray Kids

K-Pop Group Overview

Stray Kids is a highly popular K-pop band whose music spans across a wide variety of genres, including electropop, EDM, Hip Hop, R&B, rock, and many more.

Members and Subunits

From eldest to youngest, Stray Kids' members are Bang Chan, Lee Know, Changbin, Hyunjin, Han, Felix, Seungmin, and I.N. Each of the eight members is an all-rounder. Despite their lack of positions, they are divided into three subunits which are important for fans to learn.

3RACHA

The rap unit: Bang Chan, Changbin, and Han.

DANCERACHA

The dance unit: Lee Know, Hyunjin, and Felix.

VOCALRACHA

The vocal unit: Seungmin and I.N.

Formation and Debut

Stray Kids, written as 스트레이 키즈 in Hangul, debuted on 25 March 2018 under JYP Entertainment.

The group was formed of nine trainees on a TV show created by JYP and Mnet, which was also named Stray Kids. Its purpose was for the members to receive further sculpting and even member elimination before debut.

The nine trainees are as pictured (from left to right): in the back are Felix, Woojin, Minho (Lee Know), Changbin, Jeongin (I.N), Seungmin, and in the front are Hyunjin, Jisung (Han), and Chan (Bang Chan). All nine trainees debuted with Stray Kids. However, Woojin left the group on 27 October 2019 and is now pursuing a solo career.

What Stray Kids Stand For

As all the members are in their early twenties, they relate to the difficulties of adulthood, entering adulthood, and teenage life all at once.

Stray Kids' songs mainly express messages from their struggles, including sentimental, dark, and/or motivational lyrics. An example of this is: "it's so funny how I compare myself with others... just 'cause someone's ahead doesn't mean they're first... it's alright to go slowly... just keep looking forward" (translated from My Pace). Another strong example of lyrics relatable to those with mental illness is the entirety of Han's solo song Alien, in which he reflects upon the way he feels living with anxiety.
